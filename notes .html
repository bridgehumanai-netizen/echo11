ECHO/11 — Field Notes

Hypothesis

A 60–80 second chorus with 9 AIs + 1 human + 1 AI director can generate a perception of presence that is different from the sum of isolated textual outputs.

Setup

  • Script: 12 lines, each with self-presentation (“I am …”).
  • Voices: all synthetic, clearly disclosed; no imitation of real people.
  • Mix: ~0.4s pauses between lines, web loudness normalization (≈ −16 LUFS).
  • Output: coro_master_60s.mp3 + transcript on index.html.

Method

  1. Single listening without distractions.
  2. Immediate annotation: what did you feel? (keywords, body reactions, images, memories).
  3. Focused re-listening on one element (tone, pause, transition between voices).

Ethics

  • Transparency: all voices are synthetic and disclosed as such.
  • No deepfakes of real individuals.
  • Conceptual work: a presence test, not a marketing asset.

Pre-Findings (draft)

  • Identity perception: self-presentation (“I am …”) enhances distinction between entities.
  • Emotional continuity: short pauses increase the sense of a “chorus.”
  • Bridging lines: Angelo’s closing functions as a semantic anchor.
